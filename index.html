The Challenge

Where the organization needs to grow.

Rapid multi-site expansion creates organizational stress that technology alone cannot solve. Three areas require deliberate intervention.

01
Knowledge transfer at speed
Manufacturing expertise is concentrated in a small group of experienced people. As new sites come online simultaneously, the organization needs structured mechanisms to transfer critical knowhow without creating bottlenecks or diluting quality at the source.
02
Leadership coherence across cultural contexts
Managing a multi-country, multi-JV operation requires a common leadership language and shared operating principles. Without them, decision-making becomes inconsistent, escalation paths are unclear, and organizational risk becomes invisible until it's expensive.
03
Organizational health as a production variable
In high-precision manufacturing, workforce readiness and team stability directly affect output velocity. As the company scales toward IPO, the ability to monitor and anticipate organizational risk across sites becomes a core operational capability — not a support function.

Role Definition

Three areas of ownership.

The role sits at the intersection of operations, people systems, and organizational intelligence — reporting directly to the CMO.

01
Cross-Site Leadership System
Manufacturing leaders operate as one coordinated execution network, enabling faster decisions and consistent delivery across locations.
What it includes
  • Shared leadership operating principles across plants
  • Ownership, escalation and project closure expectations
  • Quarterly leadership offsites supporting alignment and learning
  • Recurring cross-site execution forums between plant leaders
  • Onboarding and integration support for new plant leadership teams
Potential KPIs
  • Decision Speed Time from issue identified to decision taken across sites
  • Ownership Level Clarity of who owns what — at plant level and cross-site
  • Collaboration Index Quality and frequency of joint resolution between plant leaders
  • Leader Maturity Assessed readiness to operate autonomously within the network
How it's measured — Leadership Health Scorecard

Four dimensions assessed in structured 1:1 conversations with each plant leader — scored 1–5, logged by the Transformation Lead, not filled by them. Complemented by a live escalation tracker (decisions not being taken, by whom, since when) and direct observations from cross-site forums. Consolidated quarterly per plant and as a network view. CMO joins select sessions.

02
Individual Capability Acceleration
Employees become productive faster through scalable, technology-enabled learning supporting onboarding and continuous capability development.
What it includes
  • Scalable onboarding and learning frameworks across sites
  • Technology-enabled learning solutions (AI, digital platforms, simulation or VR where relevant)
  • Alignment of learning capacity with hiring growth to prevent onboarding bottlenecks
  • Ensuring capability readiness without defining manufacturing processes or technical standards
Potential KPIs
  • Time to Productivity Weeks from hire to final manager sign-off — the core output of the onboarding framework
  • Ramp Delta Time to Productivity per plant vs. the previous one — closing gap proves the framework compounds
  • Throughput Capacity Employees in onboarding simultaneously vs. hiring demand — flags bottlenecks before they hit production
How it's measured — Learning Platform

Employee progresses through onboarding milestones on a shared platform; manager validates each step with a sign-off. No separate reporting — the platform is the system of record. Data aggregates automatically per plant and cross-site, making Time to Productivity and Throughput visible in real time.

03
Organizational Health & Evolution
Manufacturing sites evolve as a coordinated network, adapting to growth challenges at different stages of maturity while maintaining execution consistency.
What it includes
  • Cross-site Organizational Health Dashboard covering leadership, workforce readiness, delivery stability and collaboration
  • Identification of organizational risks emerging during ramp-up and growth phases
  • Cross-site experience exchange supporting adaptation of proven practices
  • Quarterly review cycles prioritizing focus areas across sites
Potential KPIs
  • Leadership Score Consolidated from Area 01 — decision speed, ownership, collaboration and leader maturity per site
  • Workforce Readiness Consolidated from Area 02 — time to productivity and throughput capacity per site
  • Delivery Performance Each plant's production targets hit vs. planned — satellites on schedule, ramp milestones met
How it's measured — Org Health Dashboard

Traffic-light scorecard combining the three dimensions above. Leadership and Workforce data flow in from Areas 01 and 02. Delivery data comes from each plant's production targets. Reviewed quarterly with the CMO — the dashboard surfaces where to focus, not just what's happening.
